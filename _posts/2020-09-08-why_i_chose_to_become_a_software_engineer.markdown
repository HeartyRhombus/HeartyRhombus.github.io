# 
Well, the day has come and I have begun my road to becoming a Software Engineer with Flatiron School.

After I losing my previous job in the travel industry due to COVID-19, I remember thinking to myself what do I do now?? I thought of all kinds of different avenues from going back to school for Archaeology, to going back to a position within the retail industry, and while Archaeology I think will always be a dream of mine, it is not fiscally responsible of me at this time to try and pursue a doctorate degree in a field that I may never even get a chance to "dig" in to; also going back to retail brought up a myriad of memories from a time in my life where I felt like I was always struggling to get ahead.

So then how and why did I come up with software engineering of all things for my next path?! I took a good hard look at what I wanted to do, what I was good at and what I enjoyed, and made a "virtual" venn diagram in my head.

| Want to Do | Good At | Enjoy |
|-----------------|------------|----------|
|Archaeology | Problem Solving | History |
|  | Working with My Hands | Building Things |
|  |   | Video Games |
*I'm sure this virtual venn diagram of mine included a great many more things, although I am unable to recall them at this point.*

I took a handful of "What Job Should You Have?" quizzes, despite knowing that they are really more of something to be looked at for entertainment rather than actual job advice, but one career always kept showing up in my results: Software Engineer.

After seeing Software Engineer show up so many times, I decided to research it a bit more, and found that it includes problem solving, and making something (technically with my own hands!); there are some of the items from my venn diagram list that all meet together in the middle!

I discussed this option with my family, and it was decided that this just may be a good fit for me. And so my pre-journey began with a journey of it's own to find out how I could get my foot in the door, what school's would be available online, given the current global pandemic, and what my propects might look like after school.

I am so glad that I dug deep, and took the time to try and figure out what it was that I might actually enjoy doing, and am looking forward to where this journey of learning and Software Engineering might take me!
