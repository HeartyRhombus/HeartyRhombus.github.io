I cannot believe that the time has finally come where I have reached the conclusion of my time as a Software Engineering student at Flatiron School.

I began my journey with the saying 'How do you eat an elephant? One bite at a time' in mind, as the prospect of summiting this course in five months seemed nearly impossible at the time. And now I have landed at the conclusion of the course, having managed to 'eat an elephant', summit my everest, and defeat the final boss at the end of my video game.

I began this project thinking that I understood all of the lessons leading up to it, and that I had at least a tentative grasp on the concepts. Only for this last project week to come around, and sweep the rug out from under my feet, and leave me wondering, what the heck just happened.

I could've sworn that I understood how to create components and containers, how to set up routes and forms, and how to import all of those things in my React application so that everything 'spoke' to everything else. I sat here Monday morning, wondering 'what have I gotten myself into?' I seemingly couldn't remember any of those concepts. I began to pour over the copious amounts of notes that I had taken, re-watched re-read lectures, and did some serious google searching to try to get my brain back to where I knew it should be.

In trying to figure out where I was and what I should make a project on, all I could focus on was taking a what I thought would be a well deserved break, and travelling to a new destination somewhere around the globe (although this seems almost next to impossible with the current COVID-19 pandemic still raging on); but then it hit me! Instead of daydreaming about travelling somewhere, I could create an application that tracks my past travels, as well as my bucket list travels!

So I started 'small' by first creating my backend with `rails --api`. I set up my models for my destinations, and my things to do, along with their relationships `has_many` and `belongs_to`. My small part is now complete, or so I thought at the time, and I moved on to my frontend and creating it with `react`.

Ok brain, let start at the very beginning; let's look at this as small steps rather than having an anxiety attack about we're gonna get to the final product. And so my index pages, and App pages were created.

Woo hoo! Now I can at least see something on a webpage!

And so slowly, and bit by bit, I started creating my containers and my components, setting up Redux and connecting my containers to the store according to the principle of only connecting containers, but not components. Somewhere in here my initial `fetch` was also set up so I could actually view the data I had already seeded into my database.

Has anyone else felt like days just flew by when you're trying to code, debug, and problem solve? No? Just me? That's cool, because it did feel like my days flew by, but at the same time it almost felt like I wasn't actually getting anything done. There were times were it just felt like I was sitting there starting at my dual monitors and thinking to myself 'how on earth did I get that to work?', or more often 'why the heck isn't that working when it should be?'.

But little by little it started to all come together, and before I knew it I had a working React-Redux application, that was calling to a Ruby on Rails backend.

I personally think, that that calls for at least a small celebration; please excuse me while I do my happy dance.

![elmo happy dance](https://media.giphy.com/media/kyLYXonQYYfwYDIeZl/giphy.gif)

So now with this complete, I have only to pass my final assessment for my program; hopefully this 'boss' will be a little merciful since I have come so far in such a short time, but I shall steel myself for the inevitable end game boss battle. 

I feel as if I have now completed my five month marathon, and have almost crossed the finish line. Only life knows at this point where it will take me, but I feel safe in the knowledge, that I at least now have a rowboat and some oars to help steer me in the right direction.

![crossing finish line](https://media.giphy.com/media/BDagLpxFIm3SM/giphy.gif)

I cannot believe that I have been able to push myself so hard, and come so far in only the last five months. I don't think I had ever imagined that I might be somewhere like here, but I think that I am so glad that I have chosen this route.

I cannot say that I have done this completely by myself, as that would be a bit of a miscalculation I think. But I can say that with all of the help and assistance that I have received from my instructor, the school, and my support system, that they have helped to make this possible for me. So to Flatiron School, and my instructor Nancy, and everyone who supported me along the way, I say thank you from the bottom of my heart. I could not have done this without you.
