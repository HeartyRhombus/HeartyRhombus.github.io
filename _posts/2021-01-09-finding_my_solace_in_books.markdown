If you've been here before, you know the story, if not welcome. It is here that I share my thoughts and feelings when trying my best to build a project for my Software Engineering track with Flatiron School.

First, let me begin by wishing everyone a belated Merry Christmas, Happy Hanukkah, Happy Kwanza, Happy New Year, Happy Lunar New Year, and happy any other holidays that I have missed (I apologize, I am not fully versed on every holiday that occurs during this time of year). I hope you were able to celebrate with family or friends, whether in person for those who were able, or by any other means.

This project time around was much more difficult than I had anticipated, or even encountered as of yet. I think due to the holidays, and personal family matters that were taking place, I just was not in the mood to focus or study leading up to this project (not that I am trying to make excuses dear reader); I was much more focused on trying to enjoy the holidays, and due to that had little interest in schoolwork. I thought I would be able to get through much like I had with the modules and projects leading up to this one, but I was clearly, and undoubtedly mistaken.

<img src="https://media.giphy.com/media/mPytjcsG3XS4o/giphy.gif">

This project was a true struggle for me at the start. I struggled to remember if or when I had learned any of the material that I needed to even start this project, let alone complete it. I spent many hours watching, and re-watching lectures, and reading and re-reading lessons, just to try and get my brain to retain or remember any information. I had to ask a lot more questions, and needed more 'pushes in the right direction' than I feel like I had the right to.

I knew my idea/topic from the start: a library-esque application, with books and authors. I knew how these were related:
* a book belongs to an author
* an author has many books

I had my starting points covered -- in theory. Now where to begin in the code. Looking back through lessons and lectures, I decided the best place to start would probably be the API, since I had to create my own.

Ok, so I've figured out how to setup my API and I've got my models, and controllers in place; let's see if it works. By jove it works! I can see JSON data when I navigate to the route in my browser after starting the server! Sweet!

Onward, to the actual JavaScript portion of this project! I know I need to find elements on the page, and attach either more elements or listeners to them; then I have to do something with functions to make it do something, and after it's all done refactor it into classes to make it more object oriented and easier to read.

Having said that, it probably would have been easier to just start with the classes for most aspects, and go from there, but hey I can be stubborn and get in my own way. This was also easier said than done at first.

After much trial and error, and slowly making my way, I feel a bit like a stone gathering moss that begins to roll; slow at first, but then gaining momentum. Things start to make sense, and I truly think I start to understand what it is that I am doing. Finally my stone, really starts to gather some momentum, and before long I think I have done it; I think I have completed my project, and it all still works!

<img src="https://media.giphy.com/media/l2JdWrjtH0iWUIim4/giphy.gif">

I have to give a big shoutout to my instructor, as I don't think I would have been able to complete this project on time without her. She allowed me to ask so many questions, and was able to nudge me along, all while making sure I was truly understanding what it was that she was asking me to do, research, or figure out.

I think maybe a day long nap is now in order, as my brain has absorbed so much information over the last week, that it may be necessary just to keep it from shutting down on its own. After that, it is on to my next module, React & Redux; hopefully my brain can sustain this for another month, and then be able to put it to use in the real world!

Until next time dear reader, for now I am going to rest up a bit, and try my darndest to stay on top of my studies this time; this next module will be my final one with Flatiron School, and so the next time you see me, it will be for my final project!
