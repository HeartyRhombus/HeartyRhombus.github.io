Oh man, where do I even begin? I'm sure you've heard the saying:

> "How do you eat an elephant? One bite at a time!"

To me this felt like a huge understatement; I felt as if I was staring at a whole herd of elephants!
<br>
<br>

**Step One: Find your project idea**

I had my idea: create a cli based on something within sustainability, or world heritage sites. Alas, although these topics are near and dear to my heart, I was unable to find an API which either didn't require some type of security key/token, or wasn't on climate change/weather (argh!) and so my search began for a new topic which might be more widely available.

I began to search for publicly available APIs that didn't require a security token, and racked my brain for topics of interest to me that I might be able to use for this project, and lo and behold I came across multiple! Albeit some of them still required security keys or logins, so I had to pass on those ones for now (until next time Blizzard, and UNESCO). However one still remained within my reach:

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/c/ca/Studio_Ghibli_logo.svg)
<br>
<br>

**Step Two: Figure out how the heck to turn this idea into a CLI**

I had grown up watching *Kiki's Delivery Service* and in later years came across *Princess Mononoke* and *Spirited Away*, which these films are the ones that are most likely to be recognized when Studio Ghibli is mentioned. I had fallen in love with the art style associated with these films, and my interest had been piqued when I learned that they were all directed by the same director: *Hayao Miyazaki*.

Ok kids, slight detour! This collection of films in recent years has had somewhat of a renaissance, although those who knew them previously were rejoicing that they were once again in the limelight, and through this renaissance they have become available for streaming! It had been noted previously that these films would never be available online, and yet here they are! For those who are as of yet uninitiated into the world of Studio Ghibli I highly recommend diving in head first; the animation and storytelling will leave you with a sense of awe and wonder!

And now back to our regularly scheduled program! Now that I've found this API that has so wonderfully listed the Studio Ghibli collection, how on earth do I turn this information into an interactive cli. Well, what if I were to list out the films, and then when someone selects a particular film, show them some more details about it? BOOM! Now I have my cli idea!

So now, I've got my API, and I have my idea of how to work with it. I open up my code editor, aaaaaaand now what? I think I know how I want to do this, but how do I even start?! I stared at my blank page for I don't know how long, with it longingly, and almost mockingly, staring back at me just begging me to start writing something; anything.

I walked away and took a break, came back, stared some more, took another break, and tried to rack my brain to figure out how on Earth do I start this thing. Enter Google, and lesson reviews to the rescue!

Now, that I have my file structures created, I know I'm on the right path, and I settle into a rhythm of writing my code, and reviewing lessons, and/or searching google for the parts that my brain just couldn't quite grasp or retain, despite my efforts to take copious amounts of notes. Slowly but surely my project starts to come together, including mishaps and trying to figure out how to include some things, or how to figure out why something just is not working. Next thing I know, I have a fully functioning cli application, and I have a small celebratory dance in my office chair!

To say that this project was like climbing Mount Everest, would be like calling Mount Everest a hill; I feel as if I have climbed Mount Everest a hundred times over, and gained a better understanding of all of the information and details I have been pounding my head on a desk over for the last few weeks!

At times I feel like I may have bitten off more than I can chew, but in the end I nearly always end up realizing that I can do this!

Now I'm off to start trying to figure out Sinatra, and I'm crossing my fingers that Mount Everest will be just as forgiving with me the next time we meet.
