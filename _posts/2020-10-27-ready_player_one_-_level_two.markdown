I feel like this journey is a bit like a video game that I have chosen to play; the first levels are generally fairly easy/simple, although not without challenges.

To me it only seems fitting then, that I have chosen to base my project on a video game collection, in which players (users) can create their own personal collections.

Now I'm ready to begin level two:

<p align="center">
<img src="https://i.imgur.com/OCSmb5s.png">
</p>

I started this round, thinking "Yes, this is gonna be good! I've got this! I think I know what I'm doing!" Only to realize that I have gotten ahead of myself, and tried to tackle the last bit of the level before I even began. Thus I return to the beginning and re-evaluate what it is that I am trying to do.

I've figured out that maybe, just maybe I don't need to go all the way back to the start of the level, and maybe I can use my misstep to actually help me gain some momentum in tackling the rest of the level. 

Upon reflection, while it may not have been the greatest idea to start with my user sessions, I certainly don't need to go back to the beginning and start over. So I set aside my end-level "notes", and proceed to begin tackling the beginning of the level once more; this time to my surprise, it came much easier! I didn't have to bang my head on the wall nearly as much as with level one, and things seemed to just flow!

This level felt easier than the first, and I seem to understand everything that I'm doing, I think at least. But somehow, I still feel as if I maybe am missing something; some potential easter egg, or core part of the level, that will act as a bit of a road block. It has felt like this level came so much easier to me than the first. I seem to just 'click' with this material, and so feel as if I must be missing something, because it just can't possibly be this easy.

Alas, here I am. At the door to the boss encounter. I seem to have successfully completed the level up to this point. I've hit all my save points and check points, collected all of the rings/coins that I've been able to find along the way, and have all of my lives still intact.

Now to finish the level and complete my level two boss encounter, for my Sinatra Project, in this game called Software Engineering that I have chosen to play.
